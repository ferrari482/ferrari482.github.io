MONZA MADNESS: Leclerc wins Ferrari's home race

After a tough race led by McLaren, Ferrari's strategy secured their star driver the win. The Monagesque driver had previously won with Ferrari at the famous circuit in 2019, and the crowd did not disappoint on both occasions. Qualifying on Saturday suggested that McLaren would continue their dominant streak after the 2024 summer break, with McLaren's Norris on pole. However, it was Ferrari's surprising strategy that earned them this coveted win during Sunday's race. While most teams chose a 2-stop strategy during the 53 lap race, Ferrari surprised us and chose to persevere towards the end of the race with the same tyres they changed after lap 20. Sky's commentators dubbed this strategy a brave attempt at a glorious win, and praised the Italian team's ingenuity before the win was certain as a innovative and spontaenous strategy. This win is Leclerc's second this season, having won his home GP in Monaco earlier this season. Although this brings Ferrari closer to McLaren in the constructors' championship, the two top teams (McLaren and Redbull) maintain a signficant advantage. This is likely to change as Redbull's performance continues to dwindle and the car struggles to deliver the results it was once famed for.

Newey to Aston Martin

The former RedBull engineer makes the move that could bring AM closer to the 2025 Championship title. The move was speculated by F1 social media accounts, but was officially confirmed from Silverstone by the team's owner, Lawrence Stroll.

Piastri wins in Baku

The young Australian driver takes his second win at the tough street circuit. After qualifying on the front row, behind Leclerc, he took the pole-sitter's position during the 51 lap race this afternoon.

15/09/2024 by Alicja

McLaren's teamwork issues

Will internal struggles within the team and the new 'Papaya Rules' stand in the way of Norris' fight for the title? The rivalry at Monza showed an unwillingness on Piastri's behalf to aid his teammate in the fight for the 2024 title. McLaren have since published a statement confirming that Piastri has agreed to support his teammate in his championship campaign, following team orders and not racing Norris for podium positions to ensure maximum points for the British driver and a chance to catch up to Verstappen, as the competition becomes increasingly tighter.
